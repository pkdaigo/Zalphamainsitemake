## Release Lifecycle Phases

### ğŸ”’ Private Beta
**Status:** Under Development

When a product is in Private Beta, it is still considered to be under development. While some customers may have access, this access sometimes includes a Non-disclosure agreement (NDA).

**Characteristics:**
- The product is under active development with limited customer access
- May include an NDA requirement
- Early adopters provide critical feedback for development
- Access by invitation only

---

### ğŸ” Limited Beta
**Status:** Publicly Announced, Controlled Access

A Limited Beta is still under active development, but has been publicly announced, and is potentially available to a limited number of customers.

**Characteristics:**
- The product is under active development and has been publicly announced
- Limited customer access - may include an NDA
- Used when there is a need to control adoption of a feature
- Additional guidance may be required for known caveats
- Underlying capacity may be limited

---

### ğŸŒ Public Beta
**Status:** Publicly Available for Testing

Once a product has been publicly announced, optionally tested in the field by selected customers, and meets quality standards, it is considered to be in the Public Beta phase.

Public Beta is the final phase of the release cycle before a product goes GA (General Availability). At this stage the product can be used by a wider audience for load testing and onboarding.

**Characteristics:**
- The product is under active development and has been publicly announced
- Available to the public without special invitation
- Used for load testing and wider onboarding
- Feedback actively collected from users

**Requirements to Move from Public Beta to GA:**
- âœ… Fully load tested
- âœ… All critical bugs resolved
- âœ… Security analysis completed
- âœ… At least 10 customers successfully on-boarded
- âœ… Platform stability verified
- âœ… User feedback incorporated

**Important Notes for Public Beta Users:**
- Products in Beta state are **not covered under the Service Level Agreement (SLA)** for Enterprise plans
- We do not recommend using Beta products in a full production environment for critical operations
- Active development means features may change based on user feedback
- Your participation helps shape the final product

---

### âœ… General Availability (GA)
**Status:** Production Ready

When the product reaches the General Availability (GA) phase, it is considered to be battle tested, and ready for use by the community.

**Characteristics:**
- Publicly available with full support
- Guaranteed uptime and SLA coverage
- Production-ready for critical operations
- Stable feature set
- Comprehensive documentation
- Enterprise-level support available

**Status:** Being Phased Out

A Deprecated state means that the product team is in the process of removing a product or feature.

**Characteristics:**
- Documentation provided instructing existing users of remediation next steps
- Information on when to expect the feature to be in a Sunset state
- Migration guidance to alternative features
- Support during transition period

---

### ğŸŒ… Sunset
**Status:** End of Life

Sunset implies that there should be no customers using the Product and any artifacts within, but not limited to, code, documentation, and marketing have been removed.

**Characteristics:**
- Feature or product is no longer available
- All users have migrated to alternative solutions
- Code, documentation, and marketing materials removed
- End of support and maintenance
